Griilll's proximity pairing is the technology that lets two phones find each other automatically. When both devices run Griilll and are within range, they exchange a silent handshake over Bluetooth and local wireless. No internet connection is needed.

## How It Works

Pairing happens in seconds and requires no manual code entry or account linking. Here's what happens under the hood:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the app and tap Find Opponent">
    Both players must be on the Find Opponent screen at the same time. The pairing process won't start from the home screen.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Phones start broadcasting">
    Each device begins emitting a short, silent signal over Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE). This signal is only readable by other Griilll installs — it contains no personal information.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Mutual detection triggers a prompt">
    When two Griilll signals are detected within range (roughly 30 cm to 1 m), both phones simultaneously prompt their owners to confirm they want to play each other. You'll see your opponent's in-app username before you commit.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Game data flows locally">
    After both players confirm, all game data moves over your local network — either your shared WiFi or a direct peer-to-peer connection. No data leaves the local environment during play.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Range and Environment Tips

Getting the physical setup right is the single biggest factor in a smooth pairing experience.

- **Ideal range:** hold phones 10–30 cm apart for the fastest, most reliable detection
- **Indoor environments:** Griilll works well in most indoor settings, including homes, offices, and venues
- **Metal surfaces and thick walls** can absorb or reflect BLE signals — if pairing is slow, step away from metal furniture or structural walls
- **Crowded places** (trains, concerts, conferences) with many active Bluetooth devices may slow discovery due to signal competition — keep phones closer together and be patient for a few extra seconds

## Multiple Players Nearby

<Accordion title="What happens if more than two Griilll users are in range?">
  If the app detects multiple nearby players, it displays a list of available opponents — each identified by their in-app username. You tap the person you want to challenge, and they receive a match request on their screen. Neither player is locked in until both sides confirm. This means you can always coordinate with your real-world opponent and match the right two usernames before committing.
</Accordion>

## What Is Shared During Pairing?

<Note>
  Only your **in-app username** and a temporary **game session token** are transmitted to your opponent during pairing. No personal data — including your phone number, email address, or real name — is ever shared with another device. The session token expires as soon as the game round ends.
</Note>

<Tip>
  Keep both phones **unlocked** and the Griilll app **in the foreground** throughout the pairing process. iOS and Android both limit background Bluetooth activity to conserve battery, which can delay or interrupt discovery if the app isn't actively visible on screen.
</Tip>
