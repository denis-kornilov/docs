After the result screen, Griilll gives both players the option to rematch. A rematch immediately starts a new round at the same stake — no need to re-negotiate. Either player can also choose to leave, which ends the session cleanly and locks in any chips won or lost.

## Starting a rematch

After the result screen, you and your opponent each see two buttons: **Rematch** and **Leave**.

- If **both players tap Rematch**, a new round begins instantly at the same stake. The 3-second countdown starts the moment the second player confirms.
- If **either player taps Leave** — or if 30 seconds pass without both players confirming — the session ends automatically. No chips move at that point; everything that happened in previous rounds is already settled.

There's no pressure to decide quickly, but the 30-second window keeps the game moving if one player goes quiet.

## Changing the stake for a rematch

Want to raise (or lower) the stakes before the next round? Tap **Change Stake** instead of Rematch. This re-opens the full stake negotiation flow — either player can propose a new amount, and the usual accept/counter process applies. Once you both confirm the new stake, the countdown starts as normal.

## Leaving

Tap **Leave** at any point on the result screen to end the session. Leaving:

- Disconnects you gracefully from the local wireless session
- Returns both you and your opponent to the main Griilll home screen
- Keeps all chips you've won or lost during the session — nothing is reversed

Leaving is clean and instant. Your opponent will see a notification that you've left, and they'll be returned to their home screen automatically.

<Tip>
  If you're on a winning streak, keep the pressure on — tap Rematch and go again. There's no limit on how many rounds you and your opponent can play in a single session.
</Tip>

<Note>
  If your opponent taps Leave or their connection drops after the result screen, the session ends on your side too. You'll return to the home screen with your fully updated balance reflecting all rounds played.
</Note>
