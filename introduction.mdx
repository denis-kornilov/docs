Griilll is a real-money chip wagering game for two players. Connect to someone nearby, set your stakes, play a round, and chips move from loser to winner instantly. Proximity-based connection means you just bring your phones together — no QR codes, no friend codes, no waiting on a matchmaking server. If the other player is within arm's reach, you're ready to play.

## What You Need

Before you start your first game, make sure you have everything in order:

- **Two smartphones** with Griilll installed (iOS or Android)
- **Bluetooth or WiFi enabled** on both devices — Griilll uses local wireless to detect and connect nearby phones
- **Chips in your balance** — every new account receives free starter chips automatically on sign-up, so you can play right away

<Note>
  No internet connection is required during gameplay. Griilll handles everything over your local wireless signal once both phones are open and nearby.
</Note>

## The Core Loop

Every Griilll session follows the same satisfying rhythm:

1. **Connect** — bring both phones close together and confirm your opponent
2. **Wager** — agree on a chip stake for the round
3. **Play** — compete in a fast head-to-head round
4. **Chips transfer** — the winner's balance updates instantly; the loser's adjusts to match
5. **Rematch or leave** — keep the rivalry going or walk away

The whole loop takes about three minutes from phones-in-hand to chips-settled.

## Why Proximity?

Griilll requires both players to be in the same physical location. That's a feature, not a limitation.

Playing face-to-face makes the experience **social** — you can see your opponent's reaction when the result lands. It keeps the game **fair** — there's no way to hide behind a screen name or use an automated script when you're standing next to someone. And it keeps things **immediate** — no scheduling, no lobbies, no waiting for a remote player to respond.

Because remote play is impossible by design, bots can't enter a match, and the temptation to stall or disconnect mid-round disappears. What you see is what you get: two people, two phones, one outcome.

<Tip>
  Griilll works best indoors or in any setting where you and your opponent are within a few meters of each other. Crowded outdoor spaces with lots of wireless interference may slow down pairing slightly.
</Tip>
