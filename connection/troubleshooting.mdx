Most Griilll connection issues come down to permissions, proximity, or background app restrictions. This guide covers the most common problems and how to fix them in under a minute.

<Tip>
  The single fastest fix for most pairing problems is toggling Bluetooth **off**, waiting three seconds, then turning it back **on** — on both phones simultaneously.
</Tip>

## Common Issues

<Accordion title="Phones aren't detecting each other">
  Before troubleshooting further, run through this checklist on **both** devices:

  - Bluetooth is turned **on**
  - Both players are on the **Find Opponent** screen — not the home screen or any other view
  - Phones are within **30 cm** of each other
  - Neither phone has **Low Power Mode** (iOS) or **Battery Saver** (Android) enabled — these modes throttle BLE scanning and can prevent discovery entirely

  If everything looks correct but detection still isn't happening:

  <Steps>
    <Step title="Toggle Bluetooth">
      On both phones, turn Bluetooth off, wait three seconds, then turn it back on.
    </Step>
    <Step title="Force-quit and reopen Griilll">
      Fully close the app on both devices — don't just switch away from it — then reopen and navigate back to Find Opponent.
    </Step>
    <Step title="Move closer">
      Hold the phones 10–15 cm apart and keep them still. Even a small reduction in distance can make the difference in a noisy Bluetooth environment.
    </Step>
  </Steps>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Permission denied error">
  Griilll requires **Bluetooth** and **Local Network** permissions to find and communicate with nearby devices. If you see a permission denied error, you'll need to grant these manually.

  **iOS**
  <Steps>
    <Step title="Enable Bluetooth permission">
      Go to **Settings → Privacy & Security → Bluetooth** and toggle **Griilll** on.
    </Step>
    <Step title="Enable Local Network permission">
      Go to **Settings → Privacy & Security → Local Network** and toggle **Griilll** on.
    </Step>
    <Step title="Restart the app">
      Force-quit Griilll and reopen it. The permission denied error should be gone.
    </Step>
  </Steps>

  **Android**
  <Steps>
    <Step title="Open app permissions">
      Go to **Settings → Apps → Griilll → Permissions**.
    </Step>
    <Step title="Grant required permissions">
      Enable **Nearby Devices** and **Wi-Fi** (listed under location or nearby devices depending on your Android version).
    </Step>
    <Step title="Restart the app">
      Force-quit Griilll and reopen it to apply the changes.
    </Step>
  </Steps>

  <Warning>
    On Android 12 and later, Nearby Devices permission is separate from location. Make sure you enable **both** if your permissions screen shows them individually.
  </Warning>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Connection dropped mid-game">
  If your connection drops during a round, Griilll automatically cancels that round and returns both players to the home screen.

  <Note>
    **No chips are ever deducted for a cancelled round.** Your stake is returned in full to both players the moment a disconnection is detected. You will never lose chips due to a technical dropout.
  </Note>

  To reconnect and start a new round:

  <Steps>
    <Step title="Return to the main screen">
      Both players should confirm they're back on the Griilll home screen before trying again.
    </Step>
    <Step title="Tap Find Opponent">
      Both players tap **Find Opponent** from the main screen. The pairing process restarts fresh.
    </Step>
    <Step title="Stay close and keep the app in the foreground">
      Hold phones within 30 cm and don't switch away from the app until the game round is complete.
    </Step>
  </Steps>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Game is slow or freezing">
  Sluggish gameplay is usually a sign of local network congestion — too many devices competing for the same wireless channels.

  Try the following in order:

  - **Move to a less crowded area.** Fewer nearby Bluetooth and WiFi devices means less signal interference.
  - **Disable mobile data** on both phones. This forces Griilll to use only local communication channels, which can improve responsiveness.
  - **Restart Griilll on both phones.** Force-quit the app on both devices, reopen it, and start a new pairing session.

  If the problem only happens in one specific location, that environment likely has heavy wireless congestion. Try a different spot.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="I see multiple opponents in the list">
  When several Griilll players are nearby, the app shows all of them as potential opponents. Here's how to match with the right person:

  <Steps>
    <Step title="Identify your opponent's username">
      Ask your real-world opponent to tell you their Griilll username before either of you taps anything.
    </Step>
    <Step title="Tap the correct username">
      Find that username in your list and tap it to send a match request.
    </Step>
    <Step title="Cross-confirm before tapping Confirm">
      Your opponent will see a request from your username. Ask them to verify your username appears on their screen before either of you taps **Confirm**. This prevents accidental games against strangers.
    </Step>
  </Steps>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="App crashes during pairing">
  Crashes during pairing are almost always caused by an outdated app version.

  <Steps>
    <Step title="Force-quit the app">
      Fully close Griilll — don't just navigate away from it.
    </Step>
    <Step title="Check for updates">
      Open the **App Store** (iOS) or **Play Store** (Android), search for Griilll, and install any pending update.
    </Step>
    <Step title="Reopen Griilll">
      Launch the app and try pairing again.
    </Step>
    <Step title="Reinstall if the crash persists">
      Uninstall Griilll, then reinstall it from the store. Your chip balance is stored securely in the cloud and will be fully restored as soon as you log back in — nothing is lost.
    </Step>
  </Steps>
</Accordion>

## Still Having Issues?

If none of the steps above resolve your problem, reach out directly:

- **In-app support:** tap **Profile → Help** to submit a report with automatic diagnostic info attached
- **Email:** [support@griilll.com](mailto:support@griilll.com)

Include your device model, OS version, and a brief description of what happened — it helps the support team get back to you faster.
