Before each round, both players agree on a chip stake. The stake is the number of chips each player puts up — the winner takes both. Griilll's stake negotiation keeps it fair and social, giving each player a say before a single chip is on the line.

## Setting a stake

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open stake negotiation">
    After connecting with your opponent, one player — the challenger — taps **Set Stake** to kick off negotiation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose an amount">
    Use the slider to dial in any amount, or tap a preset button to move fast: **25 · 50 · 100 · 500** chips.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Opponent reviews the proposal">
    Your opponent sees your proposed stake and has two options: **Accept** to lock it in, or **Counter** to suggest a different amount.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Counter back (if needed)">
    If your opponent counters, you see their new number and can either **Accept** or **Counter** again. Go back and forth as many times as you like.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Stake confirmed — countdown begins">
    The moment both players tap **Accept** on the same amount, the stake is locked and the 3-second countdown starts automatically.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Minimum and maximum stakes

| Limit | Amount |
|---|---|
| **Minimum stake** | 10 chips |
| **Maximum stake** | The lower of the two players' current balances |

The maximum stake is capped at your opponent's balance to keep every round fair — you can never wager more chips than your opponent actually has.

## What if I don't have enough chips?

<Accordion title="My balance is too low to match the proposed stake">
  If your balance is lower than the amount your opponent proposes, the app automatically caps the maximum selectable stake at your current balance. You won't be able to Accept a stake higher than what you can cover, and your opponent will see the adjusted ceiling when they negotiate.

  Running low? Head to [Top Up](/account/top-up) to add chips to your account so you can stay in the game.
</Accordion>

<Warning>
  Stakes are locked the moment both players tap Accept. You cannot change the stake once the countdown has started — both players are committed to the agreed amount for that round.
</Warning>

<Tip>
  When you're playing someone for the first time, start with a small stake like 10 or 25 chips. Get a feel for their play style before you raise the heat.
</Tip>
